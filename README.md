* **[Capstone Project: Boston 311 Service Request Closure Prediction](https://bit.ly/3RYbWC1)**
  * Completed as part of Georgetown University's Data Science Certificate Program, this project aimed to predict the closure rate of Boston 311 service requests at the census block group level. The project was a collaborative effort with Bill Schimmel and Jacob Patterson-Stein.
  * **Objective**: Inform the City of Boston Mayor's Office and City Council on the likelihood of 311 call closures to enhance city services and potentially reduce crime.
  * **Methodology**: Utilized various datasets related to city services, crime, census data, and weather. Employed tools like Python, Jupyter Notebook, GeoPandas, and machine learning models including logistic regression and ensemble tree methods (Random Forest, Extra Trees, XGBoost).
  * **Results**: The findings indicated that 311 call closures are influenced by factors such as seasonality, crime, and location, with notable improvements in closure rates over time. The project delivered a Jupyter Notebook and an interactive map for city officials.
