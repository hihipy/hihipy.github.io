Sala

About. A data analyst who took the long way here, by way of history, international administration, university budget operations, and a master's in business analytics.

▓ // the long way

I went to college thinking I'd be a teacher.

Instead I studied history at the University of Miami, then stayed for a master's in international administration. After that I went to Washington and spent four years in university budget operations at Georgetown, working with $50M+ budgets in Workday Adaptive Planning and IBM Cognos, the only person on the team who actually liked the spreadsheets.

That curiosity took me back to graduate school for a master's in business analytics. Then back to Miami, this time at the medical school's Dean's Office, where I now build the dashboards and pipelines that the spreadsheet-loving version of me would have killed for.

The humanities background still shapes how I approach data. Ask what the question is actually about before building anything. Read the room before reading the room's data. Be skeptical of metrics that flatter the people asking for them.

▓ // currently

Data analyst at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine, in the Office of the Dean. The work cuts across several large projects:

A Power BI Research Dollar Density dashboard with 94 DAX measures analyzing $540.6M in research funding across 358K assignable square feet, identifying 68.3K square feet of optimizable space across 11 buildings and 27 departments. The model integrates NIH grant data, facilities space inventory, and Workday faculty rosters into a unified view that lets the Dean's Office see where research dollars and lab space don't match up.

An ETL pipeline integrating 315,000+ records from NIH grant files, facilities inventory, and Workday HR data, resolving cross-system name variations to enable accurate faculty-level analysis across 280 active PIs.

An institutional KPI system in Smartsheet tracking 48 metrics across Teaching, Research, and Operations, with automated assignment workflows for 15+ metric owners on quarterly and annual cycles.

A 105-question Qualtrics survey for the U.S. News Best Medical Schools submission, with data collection coordinated across 7 senior administrators, variance flagging, and multi-level verification.

And a steady stream of ad-hoc analytics: badge-swipe space utilization (Python plus R/Quarto), institutional rankings tracking against U.S. News and Blue Ridge, and Selenium automation for BRIMR data acquisition.
